On the Spectrum...
...of User Experience Design and Art.
This makes me a UX/UI designer.

I Question & Clarify
John Maeda said that art asks questions and design makes things clear. As an artist-turned-designer, I benefit from both instincts.

I Study Gut Reactions
A unique part of my interview process is asking users about their intuitive responses to sites: what makes a site credible, clear, delightful? What is their gut reaction? The responses I get are honest, enlightening and often funny.

Designing with Data
After my interviews, I compile responses and translate them into a sort of guidebook for that particular design process. After all, a design solution is an intuitive solution for the user. So I reach outside my own experience to find that clarity.

The Generative Process
I never make one thing: I believe in generating a multitude of ideas in order to find the one that aligns with user needs and business objectives.

UI Completes UX
It's a controversial topic, but I believe strongly in having a hand in the UI of my design. Visual nuance and delight drives user satisfaction.
